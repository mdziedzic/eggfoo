egg

A symbol undifferentiated potential and, presumably, creativity. Not to be confused with the tiny, uninhabited island (more of an islet really) in the Bahamas, which for a time some believed to be the place where Columbus first made landfall in the New World. Or, for that matter, the late 19th century three-wheeled Swiss automobile. Or the Victorian artist, U.S. defense contractor, or Japanese magazine. And certainly in no way related to the English progressive rock band by the same name, their self-titled debut album featuring the ever thought provoking “While Growing My Hair,” the Hammond Organ mastery of “Fugue In D Minor,” and the curiosity that is “The Song Of McGuillicudie The Pusillanimous (or don't worry James, your socks are hanging in the coal cellar with Thomas),” notwithstanding.
